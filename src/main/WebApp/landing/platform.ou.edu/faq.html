FAQ

Last Updated: August 18, 2014

System Requirements

We recommend the following specifications to ensure the highest quality experience while using Janux:

  • 1Mb/s+ broadband Internet connection for optimal video viewing
  • A computer with at least 2GB memory and minimum screen resolution of 1024x768
  • Adobe Reader or similar PDF reading software
  • Adobe Flash
Supported browsers

We prefer the most up-to-date versions of the following browsers in ranked order:
(1) Chrome, (2) Safari 5.1+, (3) Firefox 5.0+, (4) Internet Explorer 10+. Additionally, you can access Janux from Safari on the iPad.

Can I access Janux courses on my smartphone or tablet?

You can access Janux from Safari on the iPad or the Janux iPad app. Currently the platform is not accessible via smartphone.

Can I watch the videos without an Internet connection?

No. The videos are hosted by Kaltura, YouTube or other streaming video services.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is Janux?

Created by the University of Oklahoma (OU), Janux is an online interactive learning community that gives students direct connections to courses, educational resources, faculty, and each other. Janux courses are freely available or may be taken for college credit for enrolled OU students. We believe students learn best when interacting in meaningful ways with other learners vs. working alone in a course. Janux is designed to connect people while they learn from top OU faculty and content.

Who created the Janux courses?

All Janux courses are developed by faculty at the University of Oklahoma, with pedagogical and instructional design support from OU’s Center for Teaching Excellence. Janux is a part of One University, a university-wide digital initiative, utilizing technology and digital content to enhance the student experience.

What technology does Janux use?

Janux is built on the NextThought platform. Learn more about the technology here.

Who can take a Janux course? How do I sign up?

Janux courses are available to anyone! OU students sign up by entering their OU 4+4 or create a free account here.

How much do courses cost?

There is no charge to access Janux courses in a non-credit environment. If you are interested in college credit, OU students and Special Students can enroll in a for-credit version at normal OU tuition costs.

Can I earn college credit?

Yes. Non-OU students interested in credit can learn more about becoming a Special Student. OU students can enroll on oZone to earn credit. Note: While some courses are fully online, others have face-to-face components that meet regularly on campus.

Can I just audit a course?

Feel free to come and go throughout a course as you please. No harm, no foul. But we believe the maximum value comes from immersing yourself in the full course experience from beginning to end.

COURSES

How do I access a course?

To access a course, navigate to your Library and select “Current and Upcoming” for ongoing classes or “Archived” for classes from the previous semester or later. Books may also be accessible by selecting “Your Books” from the dropdown menu on the left side of the page.

When do courses start and how long do they run?

All of the course start dates and duration are located on that course’s specific info page located on the Janux homepage and inside the platform. Janux courses run in parallel with actual OU courses and tend to last 16 weeks (the length of a traditional academic semester).

Do courses have prerequisites?

Janux courses range all the way from entry to graduate level and thus sometimes recommend specific prerequisites. All information regarding prerequisites can be found on the course’s specific course page located on the Janux homepage and inside the platform. If you are not interested in earning credit, you are not required to submit any proof of prior knowledge.

Do I need to be available for live video lectures?

All videos are asynchronous so you can interact on your schedule.

In what time zone do courses take place?

All course deadlines are in Central Standard Time (CST). Example: Quiz 1 is due Monday at midnight CST, or Tuesday 1 a.m. Eastern Standard Time (EST).

Will courses be offered more than once?

Yes. We plan to offer most of the courses again in future semesters.

Are the videos captioned or transcribed?

All videos should have full transcriptions. Learn more about the “Play With Transcript” feature below.

Can I access a course if it has already started?

All courses can be accessed at any point during the courses duration. You can also access previous courses in the Archive section of the library.

Do I lose all my work at the end of the course?

No. As long as you have an active account, you will have access to all of the course materials as well as your notes.

What if I cannot finish my course?

There is no penalty for not completing a course. Feel free to come back to the course when you can. While it may not be running live, you will still have access to the course and its materials.

How do I remove a course from My Courses?

In the Library, hover over a course tile and click the gear icon in the right corner. Select “Drop Course” in the subsequent text boxes, and save your changes by selecting “Finished.”

FEATURES

How do I make a note or highlight my text?

Drag your cursor over the desired passage, and a toolbar with blue, green, and yellow circles and a speech bubble icon will appear. Choose a color for your highlighted text and you’re done.

To make a note in text, repeat the aforementioned steps and select the speech bubble icon. Edit your title, body text, sharing and privacy settings and click “Save.” You can also create notes by clicking on the speech bubble icon that appears to the right of text on the platform, or to the right of transcripts if you are in the Media Viewer.

How can I share a note?

When creating a note, you have the option to make it “Public” or “Private.” Additionally you can tag individual contacts and groups in the field marked “Add people to this discussion.” You can also change the sharing once a note has been created by opening the note, and clicking “Share.”

Can everyone see the post I just made?

You control who is able to see your note: what you want, with whom you want, and when you want. Public notes can be viewed by everyone who has signed up for the course, both OU students and individuals not taking it for credit. If you want to keep your note to yourself or a limited audience, set the sharing to “Private” when creating the note and then tag any desired contacts or groups in the note.

How can I see the video transcripts?

If a video transcript is available, an icon is displayed to the right of the video title on the overview page. Clicking on that icon takes you into the Media Viewer, where the transcript is available. To change the appearance of the transcript on your screen, select a preference from the menu at the top of the Media Viewer page.

How do I add a contact?

Add contacts to build your learning network. Add a new contact from their profile page by clicking the “Add Contact” button in the top left corner. To add a new contact from within the Contacts tab, navigate to the search bar in the bottom left corner and enter a group or individual’s name, then click the name of your choice and select “Add Contact.”

How do I create and join groups?

From the Contacts tab, click “Groups.” In the bottom left-hand corner, select either “Join Group” or “Create Group.” To join an existing group, enter the Group Code and click submit. To create a new group, enter the name of your group and share the Group Code with your network. You can retrieve the Group Code at any time from the drop-down menu to the right of the group’s name.

How do I make a blog post?

On Janux, blog-style posts are called Thoughts, which are tied to you individually. Navigate to your profile by clicking your image located in the top right-hand corner of the browser and click “Thoughts.” Thoughts can be shared the same way as notes. View other contacts’ Thoughts by navigating to their profiles.

How do I find the interactive whiteboard?

Janux’s whiteboard tool allows you to mark up images and illustrate concepts. When creating a note, adding a comment, or chatting with a contact, click the “Create a Whiteboard” icon located at the bottom of the editor to open the whiteboard editor. Use whiteboards as a free-form illustration tool or to insert and mark up images.

How do I chat with my contacts?

Click on the message bubbles in the top right-hand corner to open the chat menu and view which contacts you can chat with. Select the desired contact to begin chatting, or click the “Group Message” button at the bottom and enter names to begin a group a chat. Once you initiate a chat the recipient will be notified by an indicator on their “Chat” icon.

How do I search all of my notes?

You can search everything at once, including notes, content, transcripts, and more. Click the Search tab at the top of your browser and begin typing your search query. Results are sorted by type. Click “Show more...” to display more of that type of result. You can filter results by using the drop-down menu located on the right-hand side of the search entry field. Note: Janux searches for only the content located in the course you’re currently viewing.

How do I view my personal activity?

Navigate to the Recent Activity tab and select “Only Me” from the dropdown menu at the bottom of the window.

What are Practice Questions?

Practice Questions allow you to check your understanding to make sure you fully understand learning concepts. You can attempt any Practice Question as many times as you like. Results are displayed in the quiz scoreboard along with the option to review past attempts. Practice Question results are not shared with your network. Certain questions may also contain hints and detailed solutions to aid in your understanding.

How do I update my profile information?

The profile shows your public information, personal activity, Achievements, and Thoughts. To edit your profile picture, password or preferences, hover over your profile image in the upper right corner of the platform and select “Manage Account.” You can also navigate to your profile page and select the “Edit Profile” icon in the righthand corner to add to your About Me, change privacy settings or delete activity.

How can I report inappropriate behavior?

Report a specific comment as inappropriate by clicking the options menu at the bottom of the comment and selecting “Report.” Please review the Code of Conduct located in the Terms of Service to see what behavior is not permitted.

GENERAL SUPPORT

What if I'm having issues logging in?

Navigate to https://janux.ou.edu/login/ or click “Log In or Create an Account” in the upper right hand corner of the Janux homepage. Please make sure you enter the correct username and password. If you have forgotten either of these, click “I forgot my username or password” at the bottom of the login page. If you do not have an OU 4+4 and have not already created an account, click “Create an Account” on the login page. If you continue to have issues, email support@nextthought.com.

I forgot my password. What do I do?

If you have forgotten either your username or password, click “I forgot my username or password” at the bottom of the login page. You will receive an email with the necessary steps to log in.

Can I delete my account?

This step cannot be undone. If you desire to delete your account, please email support@nextthought.com with the subject “Account Deletion Request.” Please note, it may take up to 48 hours to delete your account. After your account has been deleted, you will no longer have access to your notes, discussions, contacts lists, progress, etc.
